Espresso has three distinct layers when it is pulled from an espresso machine. The three layers are called the crema, the body, and the heart. The top layer shown in the photo is the crema, which is a light, golden brown color. The middle layer is the body, which is darker brown and is the main part of the espresso shot. Finally, the bottom layer is the heart, which is the darkest layer, as well as it being the heaviest.

Why Espresso?

The purpose of this website is to help explain the differences between different espresso beverages. Many people don't know what makes each beverage different because coffee house menus assume that the customer knows what they are, so they don't list the details of the beverage. With this, we hope to provide a better understanding of the unique characteristics of each espresso beverage listed.


Espresso Shot Pour

The espresso shot is poured into a small cup, typically holding about one to two ounces. The shot is pulled using a specific amount of ground coffee and water, extracted under high pressure to create the distinctive flavor and texture of espresso.
